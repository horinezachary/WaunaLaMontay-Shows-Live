About our Lodge

Wauna La-Mon’tay Lodge has a rich heritage of leaders over the past 65 years. We began as Hyas Chuck Kah Sun Klatawa, meaning “the great river to the west” in the fall of 1950 (Portland Area Council). In 1960 our Lodge name was changed to Skyloo, the “land of the mountain sun.” In 1994, following the merger of Columbia Pacific Council (Skyloo Lodge) and Cascade Area Council (Cole Snass Lamatai Lodge), originally chartered in 1945), we became Wauna La-Mon’tay Lodge, the “great river that flows from the mountains."

James E. West Fellowship Award

This award is named after Dr. James E. West, the first Chief Scout Executive of the Boy Scouts of America in recognition of his foresight and dedicated leadership. Each year, our lodge contributes $1,000 to the Council Endowment Trust Fund in honor of individuals in order to recognize that they have made long and enduring contributions to the ideals of the Order of the Arrow.

Order of the Arrow Legacy Fellowship Award

The Legacy Fellowship Award is a similar award to the James E. West Fellowship Award. It is open to a registered Scout or Scouter who has been recognized as a James E West Fellow. Wauna La-Mon’tay Lodge has contributed $1,000 to the National Order of the Arrow Endowment in recognition of these Arrowmen’s service.

Order of the Arrow Distinguished Service Award (DSA)

The National Distinguished Service Award is presented to Arrowmen, both youth and adult, who have rendered distinguished and outstanding service to the Order on a sectional, regional, or national basis. It is presented by the National Order of the Arrow.

Vigil Honor

The Vigil Honor is a high mark of distinction and recognition reserved for those Arrowmen who, by reason of exceptional service, personal effort and unselfish interest, have made distinguished contributions beyond the immediate responsibilities of their position or office to one or more of the following: their Lodge, the Order of the Arrow, Scouting, or their Scout Camp.

Founder's Award

Created in 1981, the Founder’s Award is presented to members of the Lodge who have embodied the spirit of the founders and the Order of the Arrow in their everyday lives. This is the highest award a lodge can present. Wauna La-Mon’tay Lodge is able to present up to four awards each year, one of which must be to a youth member. The Founder’s Award is presented for the previous year.
